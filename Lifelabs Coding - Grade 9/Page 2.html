Different Game Genres

  • First-Person Shooter

A first-person shooter (FPS) is a type of action video game in which the player controls
the protagonist or character. FPS games typically map the gamer's movements and
provide a view of what an actual person in the game would see and do. The protagonist's
body is usually shown on the screen in the game. Typically, the player is given a large
arsenal of weapons (specific to the setting of the game)FPS is the genre for players
looking for a rush of adrenaline.

Examples of FPS are: Call of Duty: Black Ops Cold War, Team Fortress 2, Valorant,
and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive.

  • RTS or Real-time Strategy

Real-time strategy (RTS) pertains to a time-based video game in which resources
are used to construct units and beat an opponent. Real-time strategy games are
frequently compared to turn-based strategy games, in which each player has time
to carefully contemplate his next move without being concerned with his opponent's
moves. Real-time strategy games require players to attempt to develop resources,
defend bases, and launch assaults while also knowing that their opponent is doing
the same. Real-time strategy games are sometimes known as real-time
simulations or real-time war games.

Examples of RTS are: Company of Heroes 2:Ardennes Assault, Age of Empires,
Supreme Commander, Starcraft 2,and Empire of sin, Tropico sin

  • Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Games

A massively multiplayer online role-playing game ( MMORPG) is a video game
that takes place in a persistent state world ( PSW) in which thousands, if not
millions of players build their characters in a role-playing environment. The
virtual environment in which the game is set is never still. Even while a player
logs out, events across the world are taking place that may have an influence
on the player when he or she logs back into the game.

Examples of MMORPG: A Tale in the Desert, A3; Still Alive, Ace Online, Achaea,
Adventure Land, Genshin Impact, and World of craft.
